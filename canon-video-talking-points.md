---

**WHAT IS CANON**
- Curriculum execution workspace for DIY learners
- You curate your own curriculum, using links, AI curated listings, or a mix of both
- Each module has readings, watching, an assignment that produces a real named artefact, a reflection space, and a rubric for self-evaluation
- The rubric is important: not just "did you finish" but "is the work actually any good, and where are you weak"
- Built for long-term, self-motivated learners who want to find the real thinkers in their field and stay close to where things are moving
- Not just certificate chasers, though certificates can absolutely be part of your curriculum too!
- Honestly, for anyone who LOVEEEED studying something they never got a proper chance to go deep on

---

**HOW I BUILT IT**
- Solo, Claude Code, last few weeks, genuinely fun
- Enterprise PM background: Temasek, BlackRock, conversational AI company, so I approached this like I would any product
- PM process: define the problem, validate with the user (me), design thinking, experiment, iterate, test. A lot of this was accelerated by Claude.
- Three distinct ways I used AI, worth calling out:
  - Strategic thinking and critique: fed it raw intent, asked it to challenge my positioning, ran decisions against frameworks
  - Technical decisions: asked for multiple options with tradeoffs, independently validated before committing. That's how I landed on Supabase for auth and sync.
  - UX and design: gave very specific instructions, colour system, glassmorphism, interaction patterns. Not "make it look nice." Specced like a design team.
- Sole user + PM + builder all at once, which is both an advantage and a real bias I'm aware of
- Dogfooding. User interviews are next, as I work through my own modules and figure out if this is genuinely useful to others

---

**DEMO** *(screen share here)*
- Dashboard: 14 modules, each with a named deliverable. Not a completion checkbox, an actual artefact.
- Reading tab: skip flow, mark something irrelevant, give a reason, excluded from progress. Respects that the user has prior knowledge rather than enforcing compliance. Skip reasons also become signal for the AI recommendations I'm building.
- Assignment tab: rich text, draggable sections, you can cite readings inside your work so it stays connected to the material
- Reflection: private, auto-saves, no imposed structure. Different people process differently.
- Evaluate: rubric with three bands, weak, strong, ask yourself. Completion is easy to fake. The rubric makes it harder to.
- Dark mode. WHY NOT.
